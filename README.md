Game Boy Camera Studio recreates the **tactile photography workflow** of the 1998 Game Boy Camera — capture, develop, and print — inside a mechanical-inspired digital interface.

---

# Workflow

### Capture
A minimal **LCD-style interface** simulates the original sensor.  
Every photo becomes a **2-bit dithered frame**.

### Develop
Enter the **Studio Panel** to refine the shot.

- Film palettes  
- Brightness / contrast sliders  
- Live film strip preview  

### Print
The Game Boy slides away and the **thermal printer takes over**.

**Printer Capture**

<img width="320" alt="printer" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/e19e7001-7820-4df3-86c8-555a3ed42b73" />

You hear the motor, feel the vibration, and watch the image appear line by line.

### Final Print

Press **CUT** to tear the paper and reveal the final artifact.

**Final Photo Result**

# Design Language

Inspired by **Industrial Nintendo Hardware**:

- Matte ABS plastic surfaces  
- Debossed control wells  
- Mechanical rail transitions  
- Synthesized hardware audio feedback
